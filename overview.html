People worldwide use COVID-19 visualizations to make life and death decisions about pandemic risks,and to understand how it can influence risk perceptions, in order to improve pandemic communication.
The fast-acting and deadly nature of Coronavirus have prompted scientists and media outlets to produce thousands of visualizations to convey the pandemic risks and its negative effects.

This work is part of an end-of-year project, developed by engineering students at the National School of Applied Sciences, the main theme is the interactive visualization of Coronavirus data using D3.JS..
All of the data presented on this visualization comes from a trusted, high-quality data source.
